# Customizing Your Story

Once you’ve played a little, you may want to shape how your Dungeon Master tells stories. Chronicle gives you gentle controls that feel like tuning the mood of a campaign rather than technical settings.

All of these live in the **Settings** tab on your phone.

---

## The Look (Art Style)

This controls how illustrations appear when you turn on picture generation.

You can choose from beautiful presets:
- Comic book
- Watercolor
- Pencil sketch
- Lego-style
- Anime
- Pixel art
- Noir
- Oil painting

Or type your own description (for example: “dark fairy tale illustration” or “1970s fantasy paperback cover”).

**Tip:** You can change this anytime. New pictures will use the new style.

---

## World Setting

This is one of the most powerful and fun options.

Leave it blank for classic high fantasy (the default).

Or type a short description to reskin the entire world while keeping the D&D 5e rules you love. Examples:

- `steampunk airships and clockwork cities`
- `underwater merfolk kingdoms`
- `frozen north with giant frost giants and aurora magic`
- `wild west fantasy with cowboys and dragons`

The storyteller will weave your chosen flavor into every description, NPC, and location.

---

## Tone & Whimsy

This slider controls how surprising or strange the world feels.

- **All the way left** → Serious, grounded, epic fantasy (think Lord of the Rings or Game of Thrones)
- **Middle** → Balanced classic D&D feel
- **All the way right** → Delightfully weird and whimsical (talking animals, reality-bending mushrooms, absurd coincidences)

Most people enjoy somewhere in the middle or slightly toward the whimsical side for solo play.

---

## Content Intensity

Two simple choices:

- **Standard** — Normal D&D level of adventure, danger, and occasional crude humor.
- **Gentle** — Softer tone. Violence is described less graphically and crude humor is avoided.

Great if you’re playing with younger players or just prefer a lighter feel.

---

## Generate Pictures

This is the big on/off switch for illustrations.

- **Off (default)** — The game works beautifully without pictures. Many people play this way forever.
- **On** — When new characters, important NPCs, or significant locations first appear, the storyteller will create a picture for them.

Pictures are generated **once** the first time something appears, then saved. They don’t slow down normal play after that.

**Note:** Turning pictures on requires a free Grok account and a small extra setup step (see the next guide if you’re curious).

---

## You Don’t Have to Decide Everything Now

These settings are meant to be played with. Start a campaign, see how it feels, then come back to Settings and tweak one thing at a time.

Your Dungeon Master will adapt immediately to the new mood.

Most players settle on a combination they love after the first few sessions and rarely change it again.
