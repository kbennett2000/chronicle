# Starting Your First Adventure

You’ve done the setup. Your storyteller is running on the computer.  
Now the fun begins.

---

## Open Chronicle on Your Phone or Tablet

1. On your phone or tablet, open any modern browser (Safari, Chrome, Firefox, Edge — all work).

2. In the address bar, carefully type the full address you wrote down earlier.  
   It looks like this:

   ```
   http://192.168.1.42:4317
   ```

   (Use **your** number from the “listening on http://...” message.)

3. Press Go / Enter.

4. You’ll see a simple login screen asking for your **secret handshake code**.

5. Type the exact phrase you put in the `CHRONICLE_SHARED_SECRET` line of your `.env` file (the one you made up, like `my-favorite-dragon-is-red-and-gold`).

6. Tap **Connect** or press Enter.

If everything is correct, the beautiful leather-and-parchment interface will appear.  
You should see tabs across the bottom or side: **Story**, **Character**, **World**, **NPCs**, **Quests**, **Views**, and **Settings**.

**Congratulations — you’re inside your own campaign!** 🕯️

---

## Create Your First Campaign

1. If you see a “New Campaign” or “Start a new story” button, tap it.

2. Give your campaign a name you love (examples: “The Whispering Vale”, “Shadows of Eldrath”, “My First Solo Saga”).

3. Choose a starting tone if offered (you can always change this later in Settings).

4. Tap **Create** or **Begin**.

Your Dungeon Master will greet you and ask what kind of story you’d like to begin, or simply start setting the scene.

---

## Take Your First Turn

In the **Story** tab you’ll see the opening narration.

Below it is a text box that says something like “What do you do?”

Type what your character does, for example:

- “I draw my sword and step into the torchlit tavern.”
- “I ask the old woman at the bar if she’s seen any strange lights in the forest.”
- “I quietly examine the ancient map on the wall.”

You can also tap any **suggested action chips** that appear — they’re helpful when you’re not sure what to do next.

Press Send (or the paper-plane icon).

The storyteller will respond with rich narration, update your character sheet automatically, and remember everything that happened.

---

## A Few Things to Know Right Away

- **Everything saves automatically.** You can close the app and come back later — just reopen the same address on your phone and enter your secret code again.
- **The black/terminal window on your computer** can stay minimized. As long as it’s open, the storyteller is listening.
- **If you close the terminal window**, the storyteller stops. Just reopen the chronicle folder, open Terminal/PowerShell there again, and run `npm run serve` once more.
- **Pictures are off by default.** The game works beautifully without them. You can turn on illustrations later in Settings → The Look if you want the world to come alive visually.

---

## You’re Ready to Play

That’s really all there is to it.

From now on, most of your time will be spent in the **Story** tab, talking with your personal Dungeon Master and watching your world grow.

Whenever you want to check your hit points, inventory, or gold, just tap the **Character** tab.

When you discover new places or meet important NPCs, they’ll appear in the **World** and **NPCs** tabs.
